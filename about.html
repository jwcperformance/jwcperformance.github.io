Background

Where I've come from.

I started out as a PPC Executive and worked my way up to Paid Media Director over about six years. That progression wasn't accidental. I was genuinely interested in how the platforms worked and why some accounts performed and others didn't. I spent a lot of time in the data when I probably didn't need to, and it paid off.

Along the way I worked agency-side and in-house, which gave me a perspective that a lot of specialists miss. Agency life teaches you to work across a range of clients and sectors quickly. In-house teaches you what it's like to have real commercial accountability for results. Both shape how I approach an account today.

The brands I've worked on over the years have ranged from FTSE-listed companies to early-stage startups. ITV, Deutsche Bank, NBC Universal, Unilever, Northern Trains, Gordon Ramsay Group. Not names I drop to impress, but because they're relevant. Working at that level means the bar for what counts as "good" is genuinely high. That's the standard I bring to every account I work on.

I started JWC Performance because I kept seeing the same problem. Businesses paying agency retainers and getting junior-level execution. Or paying for strategy they didn't need when what they actually needed was someone competent to run the account properly. I can offer both the strategic thinking and the hands-on execution, without the overhead that usually separates them.
