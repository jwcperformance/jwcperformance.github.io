B2C Google Ads

Google Ads for B2C. More leads, lower cost per acquisition.

B2C Google Ads and PPC management moves fast. Buying decisions happen quickly, competition for clicks is high and the margin between a profitable cost per lead and an unprofitable one can be narrow. Whether you’re running Google Ads for lead generation or ecommerce, the fundamentals are the same — capture high-intent traffic, convert it efficiently, and keep CPL down. Getting the structure, targeting and tracking right from the start makes the difference.

The difference

What makes B2C Google Ads different.

B2C campaigns operate at higher volume, faster pace and with less margin for wasted spend than B2B. The approach needs to reflect that.

Buying cycle

Fast decisions, high intent

B2C buyers often decide quickly. Someone searching for "emergency plumber near me" or "book driving lessons London" is ready to act now. The campaign needs to capture that intent at exactly the right moment with the right message.

Volume

Higher search volumes, more competition

B2C keywords tend to generate far more searches than B2B equivalents. That means more opportunity, but also more competition, higher CPCs in popular sectors and more noise to cut through with targeting and negative keywords.

CPL focus

Cost per lead is everything

In B2C lead generation, cost per lead directly impacts profitability. A small improvement in CPL, through better targeting, a tighter keyword list or a better landing page, can have a significant effect on the economics of the whole campaign.

Local targeting

Geography matters more

Many B2C businesses serve specific locations. Google Ads location targeting, combined with local search terms and location extensions, can make campaigns highly efficient for businesses that only want leads from a specific area or radius.

Common problems

Why B2C campaigns waste budget.

Most B2C Google Ads problems come down to the same set of issues. Fixing them consistently produces meaningful CPL reductions without increasing spend.

01
Too much broad match, not enough negatives
Broad match keywords without a comprehensive negative keyword list is one of the fastest ways to burn through a B2C budget. Your ads show for tangentially related searches that will never convert: competitor names, DIY searches, product research from people who will never buy.
02
Landing pages that don't match the search
A consumer clicking a specific ad for a specific service wants to see exactly that confirmed immediately. Sending them to a generic homepage or a page about your whole range of services breaks the intent match and kills conversion rates.
03
No device or time-of-day optimisation
B2C campaigns often show dramatically different performance by device, time of day and day of week. Running at full budget 24/7 on all devices regardless of conversion data is almost always inefficient.
04
Weak calls to action
B2C buyers need a clear, low-friction next step. "Learn more" or "find out about us" are not calls to action. "Get a free quote today," "Book your appointment online" or "Call now for availability" because they tell the prospect exactly what to do and why to do it now.
05
Tracking that doesn't reflect true value
Counting all form fills equally misses the quality dimension. A booking enquiry and a general contact form submission are not worth the same. Where possible, tracking should reflect the actual value of different conversion types so bidding can prioritise accordingly.
06
Budgets spread too thin across too many campaigns
Running too many campaigns on a limited budget means none of them generate enough data to optimise well. Concentrating budget on the highest-performing areas and expanding from there produces better results than spreading spend thinly across everything at once.

The approach

How B2C Google Ads should be run.

The priority in any B2C account is making sure the right people are seeing the ads and that when they click, the page they land on gives them a compelling reason to take the next step immediately. Both halves matter equally. Great targeting with a poor landing page wastes clicks. A great landing page with poor targeting wastes budget on the wrong audience.

Keyword structure in B2C should reflect intent. High-intent commercial searches (people actively looking to buy or book) need to be separated from informational searches. The budget and bids should reflect that hierarchy. Negative keywords built from regular search term reviews are what keep irrelevant traffic out.

Device and time-of-day data tells you when and how your best customers are converting. Bid adjustments based on that data are one of the most straightforward efficiency gains available in any B2C account. If 70% of your conversions happen on mobile between 6pm and 9pm, your campaign should reflect that.

For ecommerce, the priority is ROAS alongside volume, making sure the revenue generated by each campaign justifies the spend, and that budget is focused on the product lines and audiences that deliver the best return. Shopping campaigns, Performance Max and Search all play different roles and need to be structured to complement each other rather than compete.
