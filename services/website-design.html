Why not WordPress?

Most freelance web designers build on WordPress. There's nothing wrong with that — but it comes with ongoing costs and maintenance that most small business owners don't fully anticipate. Plugin updates, security patches, hosting fees and the occasional site break when something conflicts.

The sites I build are hand-coded in HTML and CSS, hosted free on Cloudflare Pages with deploys straight from GitHub. There are no monthly platform fees, no plugin vulnerabilities, no database to maintain, and no hosting bill. The site loads faster, costs less to run, and is genuinely easier to hand over to another developer if you ever need one.

The trade-off is that it's not a drag-and-drop CMS — editing content means editing HTML, which is straightforward for a developer but less so if you want to update things yourself regularly. If self-editing is a priority, we can discuss options at the scoping call.
