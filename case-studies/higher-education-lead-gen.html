How a data-led rebuild beat lead targets by 71% and delivered £3.5m in course income.

A higher education provider operating in a competitive online market set its most ambitious student recruitment targets to date. The brief was clear: deliver efficiency alongside high volumes of qualified prospective students, with a minimum 684% ROI and £2.19 million in course income as the baseline.

The challenge

The client was a challenger brand in the higher education sector, competing against well-established institutions with larger marketing budgets and stronger organic search presence. Previous campaigns had generated leads but the relationship between those leads and actual course revenue wasn't clearly understood. Spend was going out but the connection between ad activity and commercial outcome was loose.

The targets set for this campaign were the most ambitious the client had ever agreed to. A minimum ROI of 684% and £2.19 million in course income. Missing them was not an option.

The approach

Before touching a single campaign, I went into the historical data. The question wasn't what had driven clicks or even leads. It was what had actually driven enrolments and revenue. Those are different things, and in education the gap between them can be significant.

The analysis looked at which search terms had produced leads that converted to paying students, where those students were located, which courses they enrolled on and what the revenue value of each conversion type was. That gave a clear picture of which parts of the account were genuinely profitable and which were generating activity without commercial return.

Phase 1

Historical analysis and tracking rebuild

The first step was getting tracking right. Without accurate conversion data tied to real revenue, any optimisation decisions would be based on incomplete information. The tracking infrastructure was rebuilt to connect ad activity to actual course enrolments, not just form submissions.

Once that was in place, the historical data became genuinely useful. I could see not just which keywords had driven leads, but which had driven the right leads. The ones that turned into paying students. The ones that enrolled on higher-value courses. The ones from locations with stronger conversion rates.

  • Conversion tracking rebuilt and verified end to end
  • Lead to enrolment data analysed by search term, location and course type
  • Revenue value attributed to conversion types
  • Underperforming areas of spend identified and quantified
Phase 2

Campaign rebuild by intent and revenue potential

With the data clearly understood, the campaigns were rebuilt from the ground up. The structure was based on search intent and revenue potential rather than just keyword themes. High-intent searches from prospective students close to a decision were treated differently to earlier-stage research searches.

Keywords were selected specifically for their track record of producing leads that converted. Terms that had historically generated a high volume of enquiries but poor enrolment rates were deprioritised or cut entirely. The rebuild was deliberate and data-led at every decision point.

  • Campaigns rebuilt around intent signals and historical revenue data
  • Keyword selection based on conversion to enrolment, not just lead volume
  • Geographic targeting adjusted based on location performance data
  • Ad copy written to reflect the specific intent of each campaign theme
Phase 3

Target CPA goals by search performance tier

Not all searches are equal. A prospective student searching for a specific course by name is closer to enrolling than someone searching for general information about studying online. Treating them with the same CPA target means either overpaying for the less valuable traffic or underpaying and losing out on the most valuable.

Target CPA goals were set at different levels depending on the performance tier of the search. High-intent, high-conversion searches were given more aggressive CPA targets that reflected the higher likelihood of enrolment. Lower-intent searches had tighter targets to ensure they remained profitable even with lower conversion rates.

  • Searches segmented by intent level and historical conversion rate
  • Target CPA set per tier based on commercial value of expected enrolments
  • Smart bidding given clean data to optimise against
  • Regular performance reviews to adjust targets as data accumulated

The results

The campaign delivered the client's most successful lead generation results to date. Every target was exceeded, in some cases significantly.

Course income came in at over £3.5 million against a target of £2.19 million. ROI reached 1,152% against a minimum target of 684%. Lead volume beat targets by 71%. And beyond the direct results, a future pipeline exceeding £2.9 million was identified from prospective students who had engaged but not yet enrolled.

The results were recognised at the Performance Marketing Awards, where the campaign was entered in the lead generation category.

"Clear objectives, smart execution, and excellent data visibility across teams. A solid, high-volume campaign."

"The rebuild from the inside out approach was highly regarded. Prioritising tracking turned data into a genuine competitive advantage."

What made the difference

The thing that separated this campaign from what had come before wasn't the budget or the platform. It was starting with the right question. Not "how do we get more leads?" but "which leads actually turn into revenue, and how do we get more of those specifically?"

Fixing the tracking first meant every subsequent decision was based on real data. Rebuilding the campaigns around that data meant budget went to the searches that were actually worth paying for. And tiering the CPA targets meant the account could compete aggressively for the most valuable traffic while staying efficient on the rest.

That approach applies across sectors. The specifics change but the principle doesn't. Understand what drives revenue, build around that, and measure everything.
