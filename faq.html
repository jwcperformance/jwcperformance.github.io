Account performance

High CPC is usually caused by one or a combination of things: low quality scores, highly competitive keywords, or match types that are too broad and pulling in expensive irrelevant searches.

Quality score is the biggest lever. Google charges you more per click when it considers your ads and landing page less relevant to the search. A well-structured account with tightly themed ad groups, relevant ads and a landing page that matches what the ad promises will consistently pay less per click than a poorly structured one, even in the same market.

The other common culprit is bidding on keywords that are genuinely expensive because competitors are willing to pay for them. Legal, financial and home services keywords can be costly by nature. In those cases the question isn't how to reduce CPC but whether the conversion rate justifies it.

In most accounts, the highest-impact optimisations in order are: fixing conversion tracking if it's broken or measuring the wrong things, reviewing and expanding negative keywords, reviewing the search terms report and cutting irrelevant traffic, and improving landing page relevance.

After those foundational things, the next tier is tightening ad group structure, improving ad copy and assets, and adjusting bids by device, location and time of day based on performance data.

The temptation is always to jump to bidding strategy changes first. In practice, bidding strategy changes on a poorly structured account with broken tracking rarely produce meaningful improvement.

That depends entirely on what's wrong. Performance problems in Google Ads almost always have a specific root cause rather than being a general "the account isn't working" issue. The most common are: irrelevant traffic from poor keyword targeting, low conversion rates from a mismatched landing page, broken or misleading conversion tracking, and bidding strategies that don't suit the account's maturity or goals.

The starting point is always diagnosis. That's what the free audit is designed to do — identify the specific issues in your account and give you a prioritised list of what to fix. Without that, optimisation is guesswork.

Most accounts are worth more than they're currently delivering. The value of a Google Ads account isn't just its current results — it's the data it has accumulated over time. Search terms that converted, audiences that responded, times of day that performed, keywords that were tried and failed. That historical data is genuinely valuable and informs every future optimisation decision.

The practical value is measured in cost per acquisition relative to the lifetime value of a customer. If a customer is worth £2,000 and you're paying £100 per lead with a 20% close rate, each lead is worth £400. That's the number that matters, not the cost per click.

Understanding what your account's data is telling you is part of what the audit covers.

Google Ads gives you a significant amount of demographic and audience data — age, gender, device, location, time of day, household income where available, and in-market and affinity audiences. This data lives in the Audiences and Demographics sections of your account.

The more useful question is whether the audience currently clicking your ads matches the audience you want to be reaching. Often there's a meaningful gap — younger age groups, mobile users or specific locations that click frequently but convert poorly, consuming budget that could go elsewhere.

A proper audience review looks at performance by segment and identifies where adjustments to targeting or bid modifiers would improve efficiency.

The Auction Insights report in Google Ads shows exactly which advertisers are appearing in the same auctions as you. It shows their impression share compared to yours, how often they appear above you and their overlap rate — how frequently you're both showing for the same searches.

This is one of the most useful reports in Google Ads and one that's frequently overlooked. Understanding who you're competing against, where they're winning and where there are gaps is an important part of building an effective strategy.

Google's Ad Transparency Centre allows you to see the ads any advertiser is currently running. Combined with tools like SEMrush or SpyFu, you can get a picture of which keywords competitors are targeting, how their ads are positioned and what messaging they lead with.

Competitor analysis is included as part of the free audit and the new account build service. Understanding what you're competing against is essential for writing ads that give people a reason to choose you over the alternatives already showing.
